Admission Requirements & Study System

General Admission Requirements

The Faculty of Computers and Data Science, Alexandria University, follows the admission regulations of Egyptian public universities.

International students from all countries are admitted upon approval from the General Administration for Expatriates and the relevant authorities in the student’s home country, and after passing the admission tests as determined by the Faculty Council.

Transfers from equivalent and non-equivalent faculties in higher academic years are also accepted.

Language of Instruction

English is the official language of instruction at the Faculty.

Academic System

The Bachelor’s program in Computers and Data Science is based on the credit hour and semester systems. The academic year is divided into two main semesters — Fall and Spring — each lasting 15 weeks. The Faculty Council may also approve an 8-week summer semester, during which weekly contact hours for courses are doubled.

At the end of each semester, students sit for both written and practical/applied examinations.

The program consists of 140 credit hours. In the first two semesters, students study only the core courses in Computers and Data Science.

Credit Hour Requirements

University Requirements: 10 credit hours

Faculty Requirements: 60 credit hours

Program Requirements: 70 credit hours

Assessment & Grading System

Clear rules for marks distribution, passing, and the grade scale.

Key Rules
  • Maximum score per course: 100 marks.
  • The Faculty Council determines the distribution of marks among practical exams, coursework, oral exams (if applicable), and the final written exam, according to the specific nature of each course as defined in the regulations. The final written exam is allocated between 50 and 70 marks and the oral exam (if any) shall not exceed 10 marks.
  • A student is considered to have failed a course (Grade F) if they obtain less than 30% of the marks in the final written exam, regardless of their total course score.

Study Levels

Academic Load

A. The minimum number of credit hours a student is allowed to register for in the Fall and Spring semesters is 12 credit hours, except in graduation cases, based on the recommendation of the Education and Student Affairs Committee and the approval of the Faculty Council.

B. The maximum number of credit hours a student is allowed to register for in the Fall and Spring semesters is 19 credit hours. This may be increased to 21 credit hours for students who achieve a cumulative GPA of not less than 3.333 (see Article 9: Assessment and Grading System) or for graduation cases during the final academic level for students who are not academically underperforming.

C. An academically underperforming student is not permitted to register for more than 12 credit hours in the Fall and Spring semesters

D. The maximum number of credit hours allowed in the Summer semester is 9 credit hours.

Classification of Study Levels

Students are classified during their study into four academic levels according to the number of credit hours successfully completed, as follows:

  • Level One: 0 to 28 credit hours
  • Level Two: 29 to 64 credit hours
  • Level Three: 65 to 98 credit hours
  • Level Four: 99 to 140 credit hours

Academic Advising

Academic Advisor

The Faculty Education and Student Affairs Council assigns an academic advisor to each student. The advisor is responsible for explaining the student’s study plan, monitoring its implementation, and guiding the selection of compulsory and elective courses in accordance with the plan. The academic advisor’s opinion is consultative and non-binding, and course registration is not approved without the advisor’s consent to the student’s academic load.

Course Registration

A. The Faculty determines the compulsory and elective courses to be offered.

B. A student may not register for any course that has a pre-requisite unless they have successfully completed that prerequisite. The Faculty Council sets the necessary regulations for this requirement.

Add and Drop

A student has the right to add or drop any course starting from the beginning of the second week until the end of the third week of the regular semester, or until the end of the first week of the summer semester, after completing the Add/Drop form and obtaining the academic advisor’s approval. Courses dropped during this period will not appear in the student’s academic record, provided that the change does not violate the student’s academic load requirements.

Withdrawal from a Course

A student is allowed to withdraw from a course starting from the fourth week until the end of the twelfth week of the regular semester, or during the sixth week of the summer semester, after completing the withdrawal form and obtaining the academic advisor’s approval, provided that the withdrawal does not violate the student’s academic load requirements ( see Article 10: Academic Load). In such cases, the student will not earn credit hours for the withdrawn course, and a grade of Withdrawal (W) will be recorded in their academic record, provided they have not exceeded the allowed absence percentage before withdrawal.

Course Repetition

A. If a student receives a grade of F, (F), Abs, or FW in any course, they must retake the course if it is compulsory. If it is elective, they may retake the same course or take an alternative elective. The grade earned in the repeated attempt will be counted.

B. An academically underperforming student may retake a course to improve their Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA).

C. For CGPA calculation, only the passing grade of the first eight repeated attempts at any course will replace the failing grade. Any repetitions beyond this limit will count both the failing and passing grades in the CGPA calculation.

Academic Probation

A. A student is considered academically underperforming and placed on probation if their CGPA falls below 1.666 at the end of their second semester in the program, or below 2.000 in any subsequent semester.

B. The academic advisor monitors the performance of underperforming students and recommends placing them on academic probation in the following semester. Such students are not permitted to register for more than 12 credit hours.

C. A student on academic probation may retake a course for grade improvement, and the highest grade obtained will be recorded.

D. If a student on academic probation achieves a CGPA of 2.000 or higher in any semester, the probation status will be lifted.

E. A student will be dismissed from the Faculty if they have a CGPA below 2.000 after three consecutive semesters or four non-consecutive semesters.

Attendance

Attendance Requirement

A student is not permitted to sit for the end-of-semester final exam unless they have attended at least 75% of the practical teaching hours for the course.

Absence from the Final Exam

A. If a student is absent from the final exam of any course due to a compelling excuse accepted by the Faculty Education and Student Affairs Committee within a maximum of two days from the exam date, and has submitted an approved Incomplete Course Request, they will be granted a temporary grade of Incomplete (I) in that course, provided that:
They have obtained at least 60% of the coursework marks during the semester.
They were not denied entry to the final exam due to attendance issues (see Article 16: Attendance).

B. For a course graded as Incomplete (I), the student will be given the opportunity to sit for the final exam during the first week of the following semester or as determined by the Faculty Council. The student’s total grade in the course will then be calculated as the sum of the marks obtained in the final exam plus the coursework marks previously earned during the semester.

Graduation and Honors

A. The college allows students to graduate within the same academic year in the following sessions:

  • January session: After the end of the Fall semester of the academic year.
  • June session: After the end of the Spring semester of the academic year
  • September session: After the end of the Summer semester of the academic year.

B. An honors degree is awarded to a student who completes graduation requirements within no more than 9 regular semesters, provided that their semester GPA is not less than 3.333 in any semester, their cumulative GPA (CGPA) is at least 3.666 , and they have not received a grade of F, FW, or U in any course, nor have they been subjected to any disciplinary penalty during their studies.

If the CGPA of the top graduates is equal during any graduation session in the same academic year, the result will be rounded to four decimal places, and preference will be given according to the criteria set by the College Council, the standards approved by the University Council, and all relevant rules, regulations, and laws stipulated in the Universities Organization Law and its executive regulations.

Changing the Study Program

A student may change their study program after spending a period in the same faculty, provided that they meet the admission requirements of the new program, obtain the opinion of the academic advisor, receive the approval of the Faculty’s Education and Student Affairs Committee, and have the change ratified by the Faculty Council.


Courses previously studied and successfully completed by the student may be counted, provided that they are among the requirements of the new study program. Any remaining courses will not be counted toward the credit hours of the new study program.
